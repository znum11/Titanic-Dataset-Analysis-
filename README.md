Final Outcomes:-
Females had a much higher survival rate (around 74% survived) than males (only ~19% survived).
First-class passengers survived much more than second/third-class. Money and privilege saved lives.
Children (especially under 10) had a higher survival chance. Young adults and older people were less likely to survive.

The "women and children first" policy was followed.   Passengers who paid higher fares were more likely to survive.
Being wealthy helped people survive - access to lifeboats, location of rooms, and staff prioritisation mattered. In a nutshell, on the Titanic, your survival mainly depended on being a woman or a child, being rich, and being in first class. Poor men in the third class had the lowest chance of surviving.
